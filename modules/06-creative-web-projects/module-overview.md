# Module 6: Creative Web Projects

Metadata

- Module: 06 • Version: 2025-11-18 • Audience: Lower/upper secondary

## Module Overview

Students explore creative coding through a small generative or interactive piece. They expose 1–3 parameters via simple controls, iterate based on feedback, and document their design choices with accessibility in mind (contrast, motion controls).

## Learning Outcomes (DigComp 2.2)

- Combines HTML/CSS/JS to create a small interactive or generative artefact.
- Iterates on parameters and peer feedback to refine design and function.
- Explains creative choices, constraints, and audience considerations.

### Alignment (DigComp areas)

- Digital content creation (3.1, 3.2)
- Communication & collaboration (2.1 – critique and share)

## Sequence & Timing (60–120 min)

1. Inspiration & scope (10): choose a small, feasible concept.
2. Prototype (20–30): build visuals, add controls (range inputs).
3. Guided critique (10–15): structured feedback; identify one change.
4. Independent polish (15–30): apply change; document iterations.
5. Share & reflect (5–10): present intent and adjustments.

## Materials & Setup

- Browser + editor; optional projector for critique rounds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Provide pause/slow controls; avoid flashing content.
- Ensure sufficient contrast; keep any text readable at all sizes.

## Differentiation

- Scaffold: Starter loop (draw/update) and slider wiring.
- Extension: Add export (PNG) or parameter preset save/load.

## Assessment & Evidence

- Evidence: HTML/JS + two iteration screenshots + short process note.
- Use rubric: See [assessment-rubric-06.md](./assessment-rubric-06.md).

## Teacher Notes

- Encourage playful exploration with constraints (time, controls, colours).
- Keep scope small: 1–2 controls and one clear visual/interaction.
- Accessibility: add a caption describing behaviour and intent; add pause.
